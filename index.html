What is a REIT?

Pronounced "reet", a REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust) is a company that owns or finances income-producing real estate. REITs do not pay corporate income tax, instead they distribute dividends to shareholders. Most (if not all) of the dividend amount you receive is taxed as ordinary income, not at the dividend tax rate.

This site provides you with information about REIT investing. More specifically, we'll provide you with REIT dividend data to help you make informed investment decisions.

Equity REITs

Majority of revenue from rents paid by lease tenants. Buying shares in an equity REIT is real estate investing on a small scale.

Mortgage REITs

Majority of revenue from interest received from loans. In addition, mortgage REITs make money by purchasing or originating mortgages and mortgage-backed securities.
